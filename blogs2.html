Be Very Afraid: 3 Identity Theft Facts You Should Be Aware Of

Approximately 2 in 5 Indians have either been a victim of identity theft or know someone who has. Identity theft is an ever-increasing problem that bears no real solution. So how can you secure your personal information?

With this guide, we look at a few identity theft facts you might not know. With these facts, you can learn how to properly secure your data so no one can get a hold of your identity.

Now, are you ready to dive in? Here’s an in-depth look at a few identity theft truths:

1. Your Personal Information May Be Up For Sale

Criminals don’t need much information to turn your life upside down. With just a few pieces of your personal information, thieves can steal your identity and put it up for auction on the dark web.

In fact, pieces of your identity may be on the dark web right now. If you’re unsure what the dark web is, it’s where criminals buy and sell information from social security numbers, credit card numbers, to even medical records.

2. Identity Thieves Target Children

While adults are usually the victims of identity theft, identity thieves also target children. In the age of social media, identity thieves have easy access to a child’s date of birth, clues about where they were born, and numerous other details that make stealing a child’s identity that much easier.

Although, sadly, a child who has his or her identity stolen may not know until years later. After all, children will probably not have their credit checked until they’re old enough to have a credit card. So thieves tend to get away with stealing children’s identities for a longer period than adults.

That’s why it’s crucial to keep such personal information off social media. Don’t disclose a child’s address, birth date, email address, phone number, and other personal information unless it’s between a private group.

3. Public WiFi is an Identity Theif’s Greatest Resource

Public WiFi isn’t the best way to gain internet access. With the correct tools, identity thrives can see what you search, post, and upload to online sites even if it’s password protected. In fact, not only can they monitor your online activity, but they can also see your passwords as you log into sites like your bank account or credit card account.

This can leave you vulnerable to financial identity theft. If they have the right information, they could take over your bank account and steal your money before your able to freeze your account.

It’s important you use caution when utilizing public WiFi. Only go on sites that don’t use passwords that are linked to your bank account, personal email, or other crucial sites.

Use These Identity Theft Facts to Protect Yourself Today
